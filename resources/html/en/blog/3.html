The languages that I chose

I don't know when started my passion for languages, but the first time that I studied a language for real was in the secondary school, all by myself, when I started to try to read some mangas translated to English (I am Brazilian, English is not my mother tongue). In that time, I didn't even know how to translate words like “o quê” (“what”), “quem” (“who”), and “como” (“how”) to the Shakespeare's language, although I spent my whole infancy and adolescence studying English in the school... but it was fun.

My second language was around my age of 18: Esperanto! I had fallen in love for the idea of a constructed auxiliary language, even today I still think that a language like this would be better for the world communication than a national language like English or French.

For a long time, I tried to study many other languages: Japanese, Russian, German, Mandarin, and others in a lesser extent, like Latin; but I lacked the motivation for continuing my studies.

My third language was Sambahsa-Mundialect, another constructed auxiliary language that I got to know in 2016. I liked this language so much that I got involved in its development, improving its teaching materials, and helping in the addition of words for its lexicon.

Later, I decided to learn Spanish and French at the same time. I had tried already to study them in the past, but 2022 was the year that I took my study in languages more seriously. I read the grammars first, but mobile apps like Duolingo helped a lot in keeping me studying these languages. However, I noticed that I was trying to learn too many things at once, so I decided to momentarily drop French.

In resume, the languages in which I can communicate myself (but I still study, because the learning never ends), I currently study , and I intent to learn in the future are:

  • English
  • Esperanto
  • Sambahsa-Mundialect
  • Spanish
  • French

And remember that my mother tongue is Portuguese.

Of course that my knowledge in each one of these languages is variable. And I must confess that although I love the idea behind constructed auxiliary languages, my main focus is on the national languages: English, Spanish, and French.

It's very advantageous to know Portuguese, English, Spanish, and French. Think well, I can communicate with nearly everyone in the Americas, almost all people from Africa and Asia who know a European language, and a good chunk of Europeans.
