My homebrew rules for Basic Fantasy

Basic Fantasy is my TTRPG system of choice, especially because it is an open source project with an active community. This system has the advantage of not being too crunchy like many famous systems we see out there, but since it is a retroclone, it keeps some design choices I particularly don't love.

I basically only play Basic Fantasy in sole mode, so most of my homebrew rules aim to simplify the system in order to make the game easier to manage. Here are the changes I made:

Powerful characters

When playing solo, I may play with only one character. In this case, I multiply the recommended level by the recommended number of characters of the party. For example, if an adventure requires a party of three level one members, I create a level three character.

Skill checks

All skill checks are based on the roll of 2d6 plus the modifier bonus/penalty, the modifier is chosen based on the type of the task. If the character must open a stuck door, the modifier will be strength bonus/penalty; if the character tries to convince someone, the modifier will be charisma bonus/penalty. The context will matter, if the character that must open the stuck door is hurt or hungry, the level of difficulty will be hard, or if he is a close friend of the person he is trying to convince, the level of difficulty will be easy; here are the target numbers:

  • Very easy: 3+
  • Easy: 5+
  • Standard: 7+
  • Challenging: 9+
  • Hard: 11+

These skill checks include detection of secret doors, any ability of thieves, etc.

Equipment

All the equipment (what includes money) is shared, so I don't need to worry what items belong to each one of my PCs, it's much easier to manage the party in this way.

Saving Throws

Forget Death Ray, Dragon Breath, Paralysis, etc. All saves are resumed to one single number that is calculated by the simple formula 15 − (HD ÷ 2), in which the result of division of HD by 2 is rounded up. For example, an orc of HD 1 has the Saving Throw of value 14.
